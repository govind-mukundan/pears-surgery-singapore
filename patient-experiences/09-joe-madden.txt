PEARS Patient Story: Joe Madden
Source: https://exstent.com/story/joe-madden/
Condition: Aortic aneurysm (40mm growing to 58mm), valve regurgitation
Location: Edinburgh, Scotland; surgery at Golden Jubilee Hospital, Clydebank
Surgeon: Conal Austin
Date of Surgery: June 7, 2021
Age at Surgery: 63
Writing: 3+ years post-surgery

================================================================================

BACKGROUND
----------
Aneurysm discovered accidentally in 2011 after routine scan at ~40mm. Monitored annually with minimal growth. By Nov 2019: aneurysm ballooned to 58mm (10mm increase in 18 months). Standard aortic root replacement was the only option offered. Originally told by Edinburgh cardiologist he was too large for PEARS, but contacted Exstent directly - Tal Golesworthy said size alone doesn't determine suitability.

ADDITIONAL COMPLICATION: Valve was regurgitating blood. After detailed scan, decision made to proceed with PEARS and trial reducing the ExoVasc size to close the valve gap. COVID delayed surgery significantly (was supposed to be early 2020).

SURGERY
-------
Surgeon visited evening before to explain that if the valve didn't behave after reducing aneurysm size, he would revert to standard root replacement. Operation was a total success - valve now functioning within normal range.

HOSPITAL RECOVERY
-----------------
- Day 1: Remained in ICU, then transferred to standard ward
- "Ups and downs in the first few days, sickness and feeling poorly"
- Once tubes removed and first shower: "things were so much better, felt I had turned a corner"
- Nurses and doctors excellent
- Soon feeling "tender but comfortable enough to be up and walking about"
- Hospital stay: 6 days
- Gradually getting back on feet, then discharged home

HOME RECOVERY
-------------
- "Naturally a slow process of recuperation after such invasive surgery"
- Followed all instructions carefully
- "Did not experience any major pain afterwards at home, other than discomfort"
- SLEEPING TIP: Get pillow structure arranged BEFORE coming home to sleep at raised position, gradually reducing angle until flat
- Followed all recommended exercise stages: very short walks initially, extending every few days
- After a few months: felt well enough to "run for a bus"

CURRENT STATUS (3+ years post-op)
----------------------------------
- "Back as physically active as I want to be"
- Goes to gym, cycles, swims
- "Feels just great"
- Does NOT have to take any medication whatsoever

COMPLICATIONS NOTED
-------------------
- Sickness and feeling poorly in first few days
- Ups and downs (unspecified)
- Discomfort (not major pain) at home
- No major complications reported

KEY RECOVERY TIMELINE
---------------------
- Day 1: ICU then standard ward
- Days 1-3: Ups and downs, sickness
- Day ~3-4: Tubes removed, first shower = turning point
- Day 6: Discharged
- Weeks 1-4: Short walks gradually extending
- ~3 months: "Well enough to run for a bus"
- 3+ years: Fully active, gym, cycling, swimming, no medication
