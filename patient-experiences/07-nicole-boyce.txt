BACKGROUND
----------
Heart murmur detected at age 2 during tonsilitis check-up. Marfan Syndrome diagnosed around age 9. Previous open heart surgery to repair mitral valve. Aorta monitored annually, growing ~1mm/year. In July 2022 aorta was 43mm, by April 2023 pre-op scans showed 46mm (3mm growth in 8 months - accelerating). Surgeon noted aortic wall was thinning and appeared lighter in colour indicating limited blood flow. Was much closer to dissection than thought.

RECOVERY SUMMARY
----------------
- PEARS is open chest surgery but no bypass means less stress on the body
- "First week post operation isn't fun"
- At 19 days post-operation: "recovering really well"
- "Much stronger than after the open heart surgery to repair my mitral valve"
- Aorta went from 46mm to 35mm after PEARS

COMPLICATIONS: Not detailed. First week described as "not fun." No specific complications mentioned.
