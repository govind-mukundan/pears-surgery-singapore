PEARS Patient Story: Martyn Jones
Source: https://exstent.com/story/martyn-jones/
Condition: Marfan Syndrome (diagnosed age 6), ascending aorta dilated to 5cm
Location: UK; surgery at Harefield Hospital
Surgeon: Mr. Fabio De Robertis (with Prof. John Pepper and Mr. Ulrich Rosendahl)
Patient Number: 114
Age: 34
Date of Surgery: July 20, 2017

================================================================================

BACKGROUND:
Known Marfan since age 6. Regular eye patient at Moorfields. Told he might need heart surgery in mid-thirties - they got the timing spot on. In Sept 2016, ascending aorta found dilated to 4.5cm (from 4cm previous year). Referred to Harefield Hospital. CT scan showed actual measurement was 5cm.

Didn't want traditional options (re-operation in 10-15 years or lifetime warfarin). Chose PEARS. Met Prof. John Pepper who brought mesh samples. Called Tal Golesworthy who put his mind at ease. CT scan for bespoke mesh in June, PEARS made in time for July 20 operation.

POST-OP:
- Woke in ICU with fiancee and family
- Breathing tube still in (normal) - nurse helped remove
- Chest very tight (not unusual)
- Day 1: mostly in/out of sleep, felt comfortable
- Day 2 (HDU): Difficult to move but morphine clicker kept comfortable
- Day 3: Walked to bathroom with nurse assistance. "First time standing is difficult but gets easier." Chest feels "wobbly."
- Discharged Day 6 (extra day due to spiked temperature)

HOME RECOVERY TIPS:
- Buy V-shaped pillow for sitting up/sleeping in bed
- Couldn't lie on side until 8 weeks post-op
- Button-up shirts, baggy clothing, slip-on shoes, straws all helpful

5 MONTHS POST-OP:
- Feeling great, scar healing well
- Sternum discomfort pretty much disappeared
- Still being careful (no rollercoasters) but almost like it never happened

COMPLICATIONS:
- Spiked temperature (extended hospital stay by 1 day)
- Chest tightness

KEY ADVICE: Speak to fellow patients to be prepared. "For me it really wasn't as bad as I thought it would be."
