PEARS Patient Story: Sofia
Source: https://exstent.com/story/sofia/
Condition: Marfan Syndrome, ascending aortic aneurysm, family history (father died of aortic rupture)
Location: Not specified; surgery location not specified
Age at Surgery: Just under 40

================================================================================

BACKGROUND:
Started seriously thinking about surgery after a young Marfan friend suddenly passed away. Cardiologist had assured year after year that aorta was nowhere near surgery numbers. Got second opinion from PEARS surgeon who confirmed she was a candidate and would need surgery within a few years anyway. Better to do it while in great health and relatively young. Set up operating date in ~6 weeks.

SURGERY:
- Surgery took about 2 hours
- Back home in 5 days
- Great initial recovery

⚠️ COMPLICATED RECOVERY:
After great initial recovery, immune system reacted. Issues included:
- Recurring pericarditis
- Atrial fibrillation
- Sensitized pain system
- In and out of hospital many times
- Recovery took 3 YEARS away from working life
- Many different doctors' appointments and therapies
- Had to get to know her body all over again

Surgeon and Tal both assured this sometimes happens with ANY heart surgery regardless of technique and would subside at some point. Cardiologist and rheumatologist confirmed heart surgery is such a massive shock that full recovery can take several years in some cases.

3 YEARS POST-OP:
- Finally back to working full time
- Enjoying family life
- Often forgets she ever had heart surgery
- Life not the same but not worse - has to balance things more carefully
- No longer at risk of ascending aortic rupture

KEY MESSAGE: "Sometimes the body reacts to any kind of surgery in unexpected ways. If this happens to you, keep trusting that your body will eventually find a new balance."

ADVICE: If you are in a burnout, consider recovering from that before undertaking big surgery if it can safely wait.

WOULD SHE DO IT AGAIN: "If I had to choose again, I would do it all over again."

COMPLICATIONS (SEVERE CASE):
- Recurring pericarditis
- Atrial fibrillation
- Sensitized pain system
- 3-year recovery period
- Multiple hospital readmissions
- Required many specialists (cardiologist, rheumatologist, therapists)
