BACKGROUND
----------
Very active, lean, fit person with a "flow murmur" attributed to athletic lifestyle. Diagnosed with Stage IIIC Melanoma at age 50, spent 5 years in clinical trial. Cancer scans incidentally revealed growing aortic aneurysm. Bicuspid aortic valve (BAV) - the most common genetically inherited heart defect (2-3% of population). Aneurysm growing ~1mm/year, monitored for 6+ years.

SURGERY & HOSPITAL RECOVERY
----------------------------
- Open-chest surgery on Friday morning
- First 24-36 hours were "a bit rough" but felt OK with little pain
- Up and walking right away, kept moving
- Walked out of hospital 4 days later to hotel
- "Really felt pretty good"

POST-DISCHARGE RECOVERY
------------------------
- Kept walking daily along the Thames, gained strength with passing days
- 21 days after landing in UK, flew back to Salt Lake City
- 3 weeks post-op: wife filmed him jumping rope, feeling "fairly normal"
- 8 weeks: back to normal workouts, feeling great
- "Nothing to worry about anymore!"

COMPLICATIONS: None reported. Described first 24-36 hours as "a bit rough."

KEY TIMELINE
------------

- Day 4: Walked out of hospital to hotel

PRACTICAL NOTES

- Total UK stay: 21 days
