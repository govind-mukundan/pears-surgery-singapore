PEARS Patient Story: Andy R
Source: https://exstent.com/story/andy-r/
Condition: Dilated ascending aorta ~50mm, mutated gene (not Marfan), BAV not mentioned
Location: London, UK; surgery at Royal Brompton Hospital
Surgeon: Dr. Hoschtitzky
Date of Surgery: January 7, 2020
Age: 45
Height: 196cm

================================================================================

BACKGROUND:
Management consultant, married, two children. Routine checkup found dilated ascending aorta ~50mm. Marfan ruled out by genetic screening but found mutated gene believed to cause dilation. Spoke with several consultants over months/years. Chose PEARS for less invasive operation and keeping intact inside.

DAY 0 (Mon Jan 6):
- Checked in 2pm, usual tests. Cleared for surgery.
- Dr. Hoschtitzky reminded him "this will hurt and feel like hit by a bus"
- First on list 8:30am next day, no eating after midnight
- Accepted optional sedative tablet for morning
- TIP: Go have nice meal outside hospital evening before
- TIP: Shave chest well with electric shaver (also wrists) - lots of sticky things in hospital

DAY 1 - SURGERY (Tue Jan 7):
- Sedative 8am, bed at 8:30am to prep room
- Made it to count of 4 before anaesthetic
- Woke in ICU in gown. Nurse called wife for him.
- Dry throat, water and tea helped
- Honeycomb plaster on chest - not as bad/big as thought
- Line in neck, one in arm, ECG stickers, 3 drain tubes to water bottles
- No catheter (important to ask before assuming!)
- No appetite for food - managed half banana and bit of yoghurt
- Pattern: sleep, readings, sleep

DAY 2 (Wed Jan 8):
- Tea, few bites banana, tiny yoghurt, lots of cold water
- Got out of bed to sit in chair (seemed impossible but doable)
- Moved to HDU later that day
- Similar routine: tests, bit of sleep, water, no appetite

DAY 3 (Thu Jan 9):
- Ate whole banana and little toast
- Mobile chest x-ray, slightly high temp (paracetamol fixed)
- Walking test: walked to bathroom and back to chair
- 2 of 3 drains removed - TIP: keep clicking morphine button for 30 min before (very uncomfortable)
- Fell into deepest sleep after morphine + drain removal
- Moved to ward
- Tried to sit up, deep breathing to avoid lung infections
- Evening: ARRHYTHMIA - irregular heartbeat, ECG confirmed, washing machine feeling in insides
- Put on anti-arrhythmia drip for 12 hours - seasick feeling all night, no sleep
- Lowest point - felt like going backwards

DAY 4 (Fri Jan 10):
- More normal, whole breakfast
- Short walks on ward every few hours
- Walked to x-ray (close enough without wheelchair)
- Echo (needed wheelchair - further away)
- Nurse changed dressing - simple, peels off
- Anti-arrhythmia drip finished, switched to tablets for 6 weeks

DAY 5 (Sat Jan 11):
- Needed CT scan for final sign-off
- Lay flat on CT table, hands above head (seemed impossible but worked)
- Scan looked good

DAY 6 - DISCHARGE (Sun Jan 12):
- All clear. Plaster removed, final drain out, cannula removed, neck line removed
- Pharmacy prescriptions, discharge letter, surgeon sign-off
- Free to go by 1pm, taxi home (50 min)
- Crashed into deep sleep at home, forced self awake for dinner
- Kept checking for tubes in neck out of habit
- Cold sweats waking up at night

HOME RECOVERY:
- After 2 weeks: feeling good, walking daily, appetite normal
- Stopped paracetamol, just rest/reading/jigsaws/walks
- Not allowed to lift heavier than half a kettle of water for 8 weeks
- After 5 weeks: check-up fine, allowed to swim and exercise bike
- No contact sports until 3 months

COMPLICATIONS:
- Arrhythmia (day 3 evening, needed 12-hour drip)
- Slightly high temperature
- Cold sweats at night initially
- Seasick feeling during arrhythmia treatment

KEY QUOTE: "What can seem daunting is in hindsight just a strange 5 days of your life in return for a lifetime of normality."
