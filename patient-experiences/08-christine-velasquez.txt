PEARS Patient Story: Christine A. Velasquez
Source: https://exstent.com/story/christine-a-velasquez/
Condition: Marfan Syndrome, aorta up to 4.6cm, family history of heart surgeries
Location: Los Angeles, California, USA; surgery at London Bridge Hospital, England
Patient Number: 709
Date of Surgery: November 3, 2022
Age at Surgery: 40
Writing: 1 week post-surgery

================================================================================

BACKGROUND
----------
40-year-old female from Los Angeles with Marfan Syndrome. Family history:
- Mother: mechanical heart valve (1995), 2 more heart operations, osteoporosis from blood thinners
- Brother: valve-sparing root replacement (2009) at age 26 - surgery was so long he "never quite been the same" (likely peri-operative neurological complications), awoke to breathing tube and arm restraints, still carries anxiety of future surgery
- Had high-risk pregnancy with aorta never growing past 4.6cm
- Both Kaiser-Sunset and Cedars Sinai cardiologists said she should have had surgery before getting pregnant

CHOOSING PEARS & LONDON BRIDGE HOSPITAL
-----------------------------------------
Emailed all surgical centers in Europe and Australia. London Bridge Hospital was most responsive, organized, and had the most experienced PEARS surgeon. Cost was ~5k-10k more than alternatives but worth it. Alan Rayner (PEARS Program Lead) was invaluable point of contact - "extremely attentive, responsive, and supportive."

RECOVERY (1 WEEK POST-OP)
--------------------------
- 7 days post-surgery: "doing better than I ever imagined"
- Physio advised keeping all physical activity "light" for at least 6 weeks
- "Light" defined as: monitor subjective level of exertion, be able to "walk and talk" at same time
- Day 7: Walked and talked for a total of 6 MILES
- "I know for a fact this would not have been possible had I settled for the traditional surgeries"

PRACTICAL RECOVERY TIPS FROM THIS PATIENT
-------------------------------------------
1. Cough Drops - keep in toiletry bag at hospital
2. Large heating pad and massage gun - for tightness in neck, chest, shoulders (~$50 on Amazon)
3. Extra-long cell phone charging cable - so you don't have to constantly get up while relaxing in bed
4. Lots of healthy fluids: water, bone broth, coconut water, trace minerals for healing and bone building
5. Guard Your Mindset: gratitude, humor, minimal sugar. "You are what you eat, that also applies to your mind/brain"

COMPLICATIONS: None reported. Remarkably positive recovery.

KEY TIMELINE
------------
