PEARS Patient Story: Sam Whittle
Source: https://exstent.com/story/sam-whittle/
Condition: Marfan Syndrome, dilated aorta requiring surgery
Location: UK; surgery at St Thomas' Hospital, London
Surgeon: Conal Austin
Age: 16
Date of Surgery: July 27 (year not specified)
Written by: Sam's mother

================================================================================

BACKGROUND (MOTHER'S PERSPECTIVE):
Son diagnosed Marfan at age 5, regular heart check-ups. At 16, told Sam needed surgery. Initially booked for valve sparing root replacement (VSRR) in Liverpool - 7-hour operation with valve cut and stitched. Mother researched alternatives, found PEARS (2-3 hour operation, valve not cut). Consultant knew nothing about PEARS, insisted VSRR was best.

FOUGHT FOR PEARS: Mother contacted Tal Golesworthy and Prof. Tom Treasure. Consultant unhappy with family going against recommendation. Took 3 weeks to get referral to Conal Austin at St Thomas. Austin confirmed Sam suitable for PEARS.

SURGERY AND RECOVERY:
- Wednesday July 27: Surgery
- Nurses amazed at how fast Sam recovered
- Sitting up in ICU brushing teeth same day
- Wednesday evening: moved to HDU
- Thursday morning: moved to ward
- Drain was most painful part - removed Thursday evening
- Once drain removed: only taking paracetamol
- Sleeping on back uncomfortable - rolled up towel for chest support when coughing/sneezing
- Monday: discharged home
- Daily walks with brother (big help)
- Not much appetite but drank lots of water
- After 8 weeks: back playing basketball

COMPLICATIONS: Minimal - drain pain, uncomfortable sleeping on back

KEY MESSAGE: "FIGHT for what you believe is right" - advocate for PEARS if you believe it's the better option, even if your consultant disagrees.

NOTE: Remarkably fast recovery for a 16-year-old. Back to basketball at 8 weeks.
