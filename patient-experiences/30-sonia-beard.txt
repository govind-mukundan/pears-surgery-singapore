PEARS Patient Story: Sonia Beard
Source: https://exstent.com/story/sonia-beard/
Condition: Aortic Stenosis (congenital), PDA (Patent Ductus Arteriosus) closed at 18 months, severely dilated aorta
Location: London, UK; surgery at St Thomas' Hospital
Surgeon: Conal Austin
Age: 17 (20 when writing)
Date of Surgery: December 17, 2016

================================================================================

BACKGROUND:
Born with Aortic Stenosis. PDA (fetal blood vessel) refused to close - plugged at 18 months via catheter through leg. Heart murmur led to aortic stenosis diagnosis. Trained in classical Ballet and Tap until stenosis prevented it. In Oct 2014 transition clinic, condition had deteriorated. MRI and exercise test showed valve needed replacing. 

COMBINED SURGERY (UNIQUE):
- Ross Procedure: aorta removed, pulmonary valve reconstructed into new aorta, donor pulmonary valve
- PEARS implant: to prevent re-dilation of pulmonary valve in new position
- These two surgeries had NEVER been successfully performed together before
- 8-hour surgery

SURGERY:
- Admitted Dec 16, blood taken, chest x-ray
- Met Conal Austin, held one of three PEARS implants made - "that's when I realised this was all very real"
- Dec 17: 8-hour surgery
- Woke evening of 17th in ITU, on morphine
- Moved to HDU for 2 days
- Had 2 blood transfusions (blood loss during surgery)
- Began physiotherapy
- Moved to main ward
- Home for Christmas!

RECOVERY:
- "Recovery time is called recovery for a reason - I learnt this by being too impatient"
- Attend rehabilitation sessions
- Understand that heart surgery is a big deal

COMPLICATIONS:
- Blood loss requiring 2 transfusions
- Being too impatient in recovery

NOTE: This was a combined Ross-PEARS procedure (much more complex than standard PEARS). The 8-hour surgery and blood transfusions reflect this complexity, not typical PEARS alone.
