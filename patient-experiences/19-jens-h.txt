PEARS Patient Story: Jens H
Source: https://exstent.com/story/jens-h/
Condition: Bicuspid aortic valve (BAV) with mild stenosis (diagnosed age 5), ascending aorta 5.1cm
Location: Brisbane, Australia; surgery at The Prince Charles Hospital
Surgeon: Dr. Peter Tesar
Date of Surgery: February 9, 2021
Age: 54

================================================================================

BACKGROUND:
Murmur noticed age 5, diagnosed mild aortic stenosis. Sporty child, played club tennis. Monitored for decades. BAV "presumed" in 1999 at age 33 via ultrasound. 2007 ultrasound noted 4.7cm ascending aorta but referring cardiologist didn't mention it. In 2020 (age 53), cardiologist said consider surgery at 5.1cm. Initially resistant ("do nothing yet" inclination). Research showed 4-8%/year probability of rupture. Cardiologist explained PEARS procedure available in Brisbane.

ADMISSION:
- Admitted Feb 2, 2021. Procedure cancelled 3 TIMES due to unavailable ICU beds (not emergency).
- Used time well: saw fellow patients recovering, maintained yoga/push-up routine
- Got day-pass to leave hospital during day
- Readmitted Feb 8 for take-two

SURGERY (Feb 9, 2021):
- Last thing remembered: confused "in the middle!" when asked which side procedure on
- Woke in ICU evening, wife by side, feeling fine
- Told everything went according to plan - pure PEARS
- Taught to adjust Fentanyl pain medication
- One-on-one care in ICU overnight

POST-OP DATA (self-documented):
- BP/temperature measured 4-5x/day
- Medication: Paracetamol, Aspirin, Colchicine
- Slightly elevated temperature (low-mid 37s) throughout first week, some night sweats
- BP typically 135/90, pulse 85 (higher than pre-op 125/80, resting 59)
- Heart rate went up noticeably when walking initially, improved from day 9
- Nights interrupted by back pain
- 1-2 day naps typical
- Walking 2x/day, quickly up to 4-5km/day
- Overdid walking day 11 (8km) - very tired that evening

GP VISIT DAY 10: Heart rate/sound, BP, wound, legs - all good.

DAY 14 - ATRIAL FIBRILLATION:
- Sitting down, t-shirt moving in crazy rhythms
- GP did ECG: rapid AF with beats up to 180bpm in atrium
- No pain or particular discomfort during AF
- Ambulance to hospital
- Given Amiodarone (K-blocker) and Metoprolol (beta-blocker) - rhythm back within 1 hour
- Stayed 2 nights for monitoring
- No AF relapse through 6 weeks post-op

KEY INSIGHT: 40% of open chest cardiac patients get AF - should not be surprising. PEARS Facebook group confirmed this is common.

DAYS 17-42:
- Relatively smooth
- Sternum healing needs patience and technique
- Need plenty of family support
- Healing trajectory was steep
- Resting pulse gradually came down from mid 80s to low 60s
- Six weeks post: feeling really well, glad he did it

COMPLICATIONS:
- Elevated temperature/night sweats (first week)
- Atrial fibrillation at day 14 (resolved quickly with medication)
- Back pain disrupting sleep
- Higher than normal BP and pulse for weeks

PRAISE FOR PUBLIC HEALTH:
- Australian Medicare fully covered PEARS procedure
- The Prince Charles Hospital staff were outstanding
