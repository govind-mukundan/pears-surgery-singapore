PEARS Patient Story: Pavel
Source: https://exstent.com/story/pavel/
Condition: Marfan Syndrome (DNA confirmed after years of uncertainty), aortic root 50mm, valve leaking
Location: Prague, Czech Republic; surgery at IKEM (Institute of Clinical and Experimental Medicine)
Surgeon: Professor Pirk
Age: 40
Height: 205cm (6'7")
Date of Surgery: March 2016

================================================================================

BACKGROUND:
205cm tall with large hands, long fingers, disproportionately longer legs. Tested for years but couldn't confirm Marfan (eyesight perfect, aortic root only slightly enlarged). In 2002, cardiologist called it "variation of norm." Later, DNA test confirmed Marfan. Aortic root was 48mm and not enlarging significantly - told had time until 55mm. Then Marfan specialist said time for waiting had passed - was at 50mm and valve leaking. Referred to Exstent and Dr. Kockova at IKEM.

PRE-OP PREPARATION:
- Spent several months doing yoga and chi-kung (qi gong) to get in best shape
- Felt he couldn't have done more for the success of the operation himself

SURGERY:
- Expected 2.5 hours but took 6 HOURS (root was too wide, needed much more work)
- Surgeon said he should have come 2 years earlier
- Woke in resuscitation ward, no pain, drowsing in/out of sleep
- Nurse washed him shortly after (felt early but managed)
- Moved to 3-bed ICU room: 3 tubes, 2 wires from chest/belly, catheter, neck tubes

POST-OP:
- Day 1-2: First ARRHYTHMIA - heart racing, monitor went crazy, very frightening
- Given injection, heart returned to normal. Fear of recurrence never left.
- Slowly lost all tubes/wires, made to sit, then walk
- Day 4: moved to normal room with portable heart monitor
- Watched YouTube, talked to family/friends
- Day 6: released home (looked very weak, wife wondered how he'd make 2-hour car journey)

HOME RECOVERY:
- No pain killers needed (surprising)
- Only discomfort: stiff neck and shoulders
- Needed several pillows to half-sit/lay (couldn't lay flat)
- Advised back-sleeping only for 6 weeks (long time)
- Physio friend helped with Vojta's method (released tension, opened chest)

⚠️ 2 WEEKS POST-OP - SEVERE ARRHYTHMIA:
- In bed, out of nowhere, arrhythmia came. Wife called ambulance.
- Heart rate OVER 200 bpm, thought he was dying, nearly fainting
- Took nearly 1 hour to transport to hospital
- Injections didn't help - needed ELECTRIC CARDIOVERSION next day
- Released with new pills. Warned if repeated, would need ablation.

PSYCHOLOGICAL IMPACT:
- Became anxious without realizing it (PTSD)
- Obsessed with food (eating every 2 hours, believing it prevented arrhythmia)
- 1 week later: another milder arrhythmia (155bpm, self-resolved after 2 hours)
- Psychiatrist friend diagnosed textbook POST-TRAUMATIC STRESS DISORDER
- Given psychiatric medication - really helped with sleep and anxiety
- Medication was addictive and hard to stop

TIMELINE:
- ~2.5 months: got most strength back, biggest enemy (anxiety) defeated
- ~4 months: felt nearly same as before operation
- ~5 months: no arrhythmia since

COMPLICATIONS (SEVERE CASE):
- Surgery took 6 hours instead of 2.5 (root too wide)
- Multiple arrhythmia episodes (including one requiring cardioversion)
- Post-traumatic stress disorder
- Anxiety
- Neck/shoulder stiffness

KEY INSIGHT: Physical AND emotional recovery from chest surgery. "I can see now how much I was put out of balance by the cut of my chest, not just physically but also emotionally."
