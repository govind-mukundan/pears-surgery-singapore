PEARS Patient Story: Joshua Tullett
Source: https://exstent.com/story/joshua-tullett/
Condition: Marfan Syndrome, dilated aorta (family history - grandad died young, uncle had 2 operations)
Location: London, UK; surgery at St Thomas' and Guy's Hospital
Surgeon: Conal Austin
Age: 21
Date of Surgery: October 16, 2018

================================================================================

BACKGROUND:
21-year-old university graduate, Marfan syndrome runs in family. Active in sports (cricket 16 years, hockey 7, golf 4). Always felt Marfan hindered him but never expressed it. Family history: grandad died young from aneurysm, uncle had 2 major heart operations. PEARS was preventive measure.

In 2017 (3rd year university), told surgery needed. Was devastated - "couldn't believe as an active 20 year old" needing open heart surgery. Fell apart emotionally. Originally scheduled at Royal Brompton but moved to St Thomas under Conal Austin.

SURGERY WEEK:
Monday 15th: Admitted. Felt out of place as youngest by ~40 years. Still couldn't confirm surgery would happen next day (elective surgery can be bumped by emergencies). Slept surprisingly well due to exhaustion.

Tuesday 16th (Surgery): Still not confirmed at 7:30am. "One of the worst waits." 8:22am finally taken. Pre-op emotional but relieved. Surgery successful.

Tuesday afternoon: Woke in ICU. Breathing tube removed (no memory of it). Sore, lethargic, pain. Fentanyl plus other drugs helped. Slept most of day.

Wednesday (Day 1): Moved to regular ward (skipped HDU - great progress). Chest drain removed. Got temp spike. Out of bed sitting in chair.

Thursday (Day 2): Walking to toilet 48 hours after surgery. Visitors came. Walked around ward with girlfriend. Felt tired and sore but progressing well. Chest felt like "pulling apart like a cheese-string" if moved wrong. Sleeping well once out of ICU.

Friday (Day 3): Cannulas and neck line removed. Walking more easily. First shower - "weight lifted off shoulders." Slight temperature and elevated heart rate.

Saturday (Day 4) - DISCHARGE: Temperature settled. Walked out. Took train home (opted against taxi due to Brexit march traffic). Walking to Waterloo station(!).

RECOVERY (6.5 weeks):
- Scar healing extremely well, quicker than anticipated
- First cardiac rehab session at 6 weeks (2x/week sessions offered)
- Restricted physically; used adult colouring books to stay occupied
- Heart beat louder and harder than normal post-op (normal reaction)
- Heart rate quicker than pre-op (settles over time)

COMPLICATIONS:
- Temperature spike (day 1)
- Elevated heart rate (settled over time)
- Louder heartbeat (normal post-op)

TOP TIPS:
1. Don't bottle up feelings mentally pre/post-surgery
2. Don't over-exert in recovery
3. Don't lie about physical limitations
4. Do post-op breathing and walking exercises
