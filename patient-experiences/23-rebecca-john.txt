PEARS Patient Story: Rebecca John
Source: https://exstent.com/story/rebecca-john/
Condition: Marfan Syndrome, dilated aorta
Location: London, UK; surgery at Evelina London Children's Hospital
Surgeon: Dr. Conal Austin
Age: 15 (16 when writing)
Date of Surgery: September 6, 2018

================================================================================

BACKGROUND:
16-year-old with Marfan Syndrome and dilated aorta. Had prior eye operation so knew what anaesthesia was like. Worried about missing school (had GCSEs that year). Read up on PEARS procedure before surgery.

SURGERY AND RECOVERY:
- First memories in ICU with parents asking if she was okay
- Had been conscious before but no memory of it
- Chest tube removed shortly after waking - "very painful but only for the short while"
- Given morphine button for pain relief
- Next morning: stitches removed, spent most of day in bed (exhausted)
- Evening: catheter removed
- Next day: moved to children's ward, started small walks, more energy
- Difficult to get up due to strain on torso
- After showing she could walk and final checks: DISCHARGED after 4 days

HOME RECOVERY:
- Rested at home for 2 weeks
- Chest pain quickly fading
- By end of week 2: already attending lessons
- Got GCSE results successfully, attending college with no physical difficulties
- Scar already faded, no longer self-conscious

COMPLICATIONS: Minimal - just initial pain and exhaustion

NOTE: Very rapid recovery. 4-day hospital stay. Back at school in 2 weeks. One of the youngest patients featured.
