PEARS Patient Story: Raquel Adami
Source: https://exstent.com/story/raquel-adami/
Condition: Marfan Syndrome, aortic root increased from 3.7cm to 5cm, prior scoliosis surgery
Location: Sao Paulo, Brazil; surgery at Hospital Divina Providencia, Porto Alegre, Brazil
Surgeon: Dr. Renato Kalil (with Dr. Conal Austin teaching the technique)
Patient Number: 593 (one of first 3 PEARS patients in the Americas)
Date of Surgery: March 9, 2022

================================================================================

BACKGROUND:
Known Marfan since baby. Annual check-ups with mother. Aortic root increased from 3.7cm to 4.3cm in 2016, started betablockers. Had scoliosis operation in 2018 with good recovery.

In 2021, mother had aortic dissection and did not survive 12-hour surgery. 3 months later, aneurysm had increased to 5cm in 6 months. Scared at 30 years old. Remembered posts about PEARS, contacted ExoVasc.

Got in touch with Tal who forwarded to London hospital. Cost was prohibitive without insurance. Later, a PEARS clinical trial was being set up in Brazil. Despite COVID delays, stayed positive.

Had CT scan issues due to arrhythmia - changed from betablocker to anti-arrhythmic medication, repeated CT after 15 days.

SURGERY DAY (March 9, 2022):
- Went to surgery room at 1pm
- Left surgery ~4 hours later, saying few words to dad
- Officially woke ~10pm in ICU, thirst, no chest pain
- Got water, slept again. Woke with back pain, transferred to armchair.

POST-OP DAY 1:
- Echocardiogram and x-ray - everything looked well
- Tired, short of breath, still no pain
- Well assisted by nutritionists and physiotherapists
- Back pain managed with medication and armchair transfers
- Had nausea from one pain medication - suspended part of them

POST-OP DAY 2:
- Could take a shower by herself despite shortness of breath
- Visits from cardiologists and Dr. Kalil - drain was good
- Dr. Austin and Tal visited - surgery had been straightforward
- Told would transfer to private room next day

POST-OP DAYS 3-4:
- Recovery much better in private room
- Could have family with her
- Walking through hospital, eating better food, taking less medication
- Told would leave hospital next day

LAST HOSPITAL DAY:
- X-ray, blood tests, echocardiogram - everything well
- Left hospital ~3pm
- Recommendations: no heavy things, careful getting up from bed, medication, CT scan in 1 month

HOME RECOVERY:
- Spent one more week in hotel, short walks and reading, then flew back to Sao Paulo
- First month was challenging: family supported daily tasks, hard to sleep well due to position, still tired
- First CT scan showed prosthesis well but pleural effusion - treated with diuretics (only complication)
- 2 months post-surgery: returned to Pilates classes and longer walks

COMPLICATIONS:
- Nausea from pain medication (day 1)
- Pleural effusion (found on 1-month CT scan, treated with diuretics)

KEY QUOTE: "PEARS changed my life, now I can have other plans and dreams for my life."
