PEARS Patient Story: James
Source: https://exstent.com/story/james/
Condition: Marfan Syndrome, aortic root ~4.6cm
Location: London, UK; surgery at Royal Brompton Hospital
Surgeon: Mr. Petrou
Date of Surgery: Mid-November 2020
Age: Mid 30s

================================================================================

BACKGROUND:
Known Marfan due to family history. Aortic root slowly increased over a decade past 4.5cm to ~4.6cm. Consulted Dr Anne Child at Marfan Trust (strongly recommended). Chose PEARS due to shorter recovery, no lifelong warfarin. Mr Petrou experienced in both PEARS and root replacement - could switch if needed.

PRE-OP:
- Got fitter before surgery: jogging 2x/week, yoga daily. Helped calm, focus, and recovery. Nurse commented on low pulse rate.
- 4.5 month wait from consultation to surgery (stressful due to COVID uncertainty)
- Brought books, audiobooks, podcasts, music

SURGERY:
- Fasted from Monday night, surgery Tuesday. Said "I feel a bit nervous" - anaesthetist used as cue to knock him out.
- Only wrist cannula before anaesthetic, other lines go in after.

POST-OP:
- Woke up, told operation successful. Everything very bright, eyesight worse than usual for couple of days (returned to normal).
- Finished breakfast next morning then promptly threw up on ward round doctors.
- Progression: recovery → HDU → ward. At each stage more wires removed, less pain relief.
- Self-administered pain clicker, then pills and oramorph.

MENTAL WELLBEING WARNING:
- Medication caused very unpleasant, lucid dreams/nightmares - couldn't sleep some nights
- Losartan before bed was the cause (normally takes it in the morning)
- Classical music and audiobooks helped pass difficult nights

CENTRAL LINE (NECK) ISSUE:
- Hard to keep head/neck in normal posture, line painfully moved around
- Ended up leaning head to one side - contributed to neck pain for next month
- TIP: Ask nurses to tape it up to jaw area early - reduces pain significantly

RECOVERY:
- Left on day 4. Got black cab from outside hospital.
- FEW DAYS HOME: Woke with rapid irregular heartbeat - ATRIAL FIBRILLATION
- Should have gone straight to A&E but went via GP first (due to COVID nervousness)
- GP did ECG → ambulance → hospital re-sus → given amiodarone → sorted quickly
- Stayed 2 nights. Highlight: nurses sang happy birthday at his bed!
- Put on beta blockers (should have been prescribed post-surgery originally)

HOME PRACTICAL TIPS:
- Anti-slip mat in shower (felt unstable first few weeks)
- Big pillows for slightly elevated sleeping angle
- Pain of operation subsided fairly quickly, then neck/back pain took over
- For longer walks: dress very warm, 2+ extra layers than normal
- Snood to keep neck warm (wore all the time, really helped)
- Continually hungry first month - get lots of calories, guilt-free treats
- 8 weeks: quite long walks (several hours), short light fitness routine
- Frequent fatigue, needed regular naps (reduced a lot by week 8)
- Expected ~3 months before part-time work

COMPLICATIONS:
- Lucid nightmares from medication
- Atrial fibrillation (few days after discharge, needed hospital readmission)
- Neck/back pain for a month+ (partly from central line posture)
- Fatigue requiring naps

NOTE FOR LIVING ALONE: Had caring responsibilities and was in pandemic. Anti-slip mat, warm clothing, elevated sleeping are practical solo-living tips.
