PEARS Patient Story: Dennis Lieskamp
Source: https://exstent.com/story/dennis-lieskamp/
Condition: Ascending aorta aneurysm (unknown cause, NOT Marfan)
Location: Netherlands; surgery at Radboud University Hospital
Age at Surgery: 36
Writing: 5 months post-surgery

================================================================================

BACKGROUND
----------
Aneurysm discovered by chance during CT scan for a persistent cough. Age 36, healthy, no known connective tissue disorder. Genetic testing of 54 known genes came back negative.

HOSPITAL RECOVERY
-----------------
Surgery went well. Hospital days were "much harder than expected" - pain, sickness, misery. Discharged after 6 days.

HOME RECOVERY
-------------
Day one home: started 2 short walks/day, quickly progressed to longer distances. Felt better and stronger every day.

MAJOR COMPLICATION
------------------
3 weeks after discharge: readmitted with atrial fibrillation caused by pericardial effusion (fluid around heart). Required drain insertion between ribs into pericardium (described as "most distressing moment"). Temporary medication for arrhythmia which resolved on its own.

5 MONTHS POST-SURGERY
----------------------
Rehabilitation completed, off all medication, doing very well. Full confidence in body restored.

KEY RECOVERY TIMELINE
---------------------
- Days 1-6: Hospital, pain/sickness
- Day 6: Discharged
- Week 3: READMITTED (atrial fibrillation/pericardial effusion)
- 5 months: Fully recovered, no medication

SUPPORT: Girlfriend present. 6-day hospital stay. Readmission at week 3 is critical for solo patients to plan for.
