PEARS Patient Story: Jindrich
Source: https://exstent.com/story/henry/
Condition: Bicuspid aortic valve (BAV) with 5.1cm ascending aortic aneurysm
Location: Prague, Czech Republic; surgery at General University Hospital Prague
Surgeon: Prof. Tomas Grus
Patient Number: 636
Date of Surgery: May 26, 2022
Age at Surgery: 21

================================================================================

BACKGROUND:
Fell in love with fitness/bodybuilding in early teens. Chose college of physical education, started training MMA with 2-3 sessions 5x/week. After one tough workout, felt strong chest pain and fainted on subway. Ex-girlfriend convinced him to call ambulance. CT scan and echo discovered bicuspid aortic valve and 5.1cm ascending aortic aneurysm. Sports career fell apart overnight.

JOURNEY TO PEARS:
- Initially offered PEARS as go-to option because young and valve works well
- After 3 months, surgeon changed mind and preferred valve sparing root replacement (VSSR) due to BAV
- Fell into depression, anxiety, panic attacks - afraid to be home alone or lift >5kg
- Agreed to VSSR, got surgery date
- Day before surgery, team called to say they changed minds back to PEARS
- Had to wait another 5 weeks for device

SURGERY AND HOSPITAL STAY:
- Day 1: Shaved and prepared
- Day 2: Surgery early morning. Sleeping pills night before, sedative in morning. Surgeon visited and assured best option.
- Day 2-4: Woke in ICU, don't remember much. First meal day 3. Pain was almost non-existent - just discomfort from drain tubes.
- Day 4: Moved to normal room. Started eating, physiotherapy 2x/day, walks around corridor. Small pericardial effusion (disappeared day 5) and slight temperature.
- Day 6: Drain tubes removed - huge relief
- Day 8: Released home with medication and wound care instructions

HOME RECOVERY:
- Went well, dealt with some atrial fibrillation (managed with higher dose bisoprolol)
- Daily walks morning and evening, felt stronger day by day
- Brain fog from anesthesia clearing
- After 3 months: almost back at physical condition, started swimming 3x/week, yoga, 4-5km walks
- Changed lifestyle: regular aerobic exercise, healthy diet, quality sleep

LIFE AFTER PEARS (1.5 years post-op):
- Aortic dimensions: went from 5.1 to 4.2cm post-op
- Mild regurgitation of BAV disappeared
- Lost 15kg muscle but feels better than ever
- Takes bisoprolol 2.5mg daily as prevention
- BP under 120/70, pulse low 60s
- Feels mentally tough, resilient, more mature
- Started IT college, found part-time IT job

COMPLICATIONS:
- Small pericardial effusion (resolved by day 5)
- Slight temperature (resolved by day 5)
- Atrial fibrillation at home (managed with higher bisoprolol dose)
- Depression/anxiety pre-surgery

TIPS:
- Do your own research, get second opinion, don't be afraid to ask questions
- Don't panic about your condition - knowing about it is a blessing
- Don't be afraid of the procedure - after some time it will just be a week in your life
