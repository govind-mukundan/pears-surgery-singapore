PEARS Patient Story: Frances Sedgwick
Source: https://exstent.com/story/frances-sedgwick/
Condition: Marfan Syndrome, dilated aortic root 46-47mm, mitral valve prolapse, M.E./CFS, needle phobic
Location: UK; surgery at Guy's and St Thomas' Hospital, London
Surgeon: Mr. Conal Austin
Patient Number: 299
Date of Surgery: February 4, 2020

================================================================================

BACKGROUND:
Diagnosed with Marfan as teenager. Sister died suddenly of aortic dissection in 2018 aged 52. MRI showed aortic root 46mm, then 47mm a year later. Also has M.E. (Chronic Fatigue Syndrome) which complicated recovery significantly. Very needle phobic. Never had operation before.

IMPORTANT NOTE: Recovery was more challenging than typical due to M.E./CFS comorbidity.

DAY BEFORE OPERATION:
- Drove to London day before, pre-op meetings with Mr. Austin
- Signed consent form, blood tests including genetic testing
- Given Hibiscrub to shower in
- Ate meal overlooking London Eye, surprisingly calm (possibly Diazepam)

OPERATION DAY (Feb 4, 2020):
- First on list, arrived 6:30am
- Became scared/tearful when anaesthetist came - given Temazepam
- Hot air blanket on trolley (lovely as she feels cold easily)
- Doesn't remember cannulation - Emla cream worked
- Surgery went well - ExoVasc fitted around root and ascending aorta
- Suffered postoperative hypothermia - stayed on ventilator 11 hours while warmed up
- Ventilator removed after ~6pm, wasn't unpleasant
- Felt sick once - immediately given anti-emetic
- M.E. symptoms significantly impacted: limbs felt like lead, couldn't move

DAY 2 (Feb 5):
- Moved to High Dependency Unit
- Still too weak to mobilise into chair (M.E. impact)
- Nurse hand fed her soup

DAY 3 (Feb 6):
- Helped into chair
- Chest drain removed - rather painful, aided by PCA Fentanyl
- Catheter removed
- Transferred to Doulton Cardiac Ward, taken for x-ray
- Mr. Austin showed video of heart with new jacket - fascinating

DAY 4 (Feb 7):
- Temperature spiked overnight
- Diagnosed with Pericarditis - treated with Ibuprofen
- PCA/background pain relief cannula removed, switched to oral painkillers
- Central line removed from neck (uncomfortable)
- Chest dressing removed revealing long, neat scar
- Visited by Tal Golesworthy

DAYS 5-8 (Feb 8-11):
- Struggled without intravenous pain relief
- Exhaustion and weakness severe, became tearful
- Unable to sit up unaided or walk unassisted
- Considerable pain in legs and calf muscles
- Ankles and feet swollen to twice normal size
- Physio helped walk with walking frame
- Discussed extending discharge a few days
- 3 nights of insomnia (unusual for her)
- Used night time wisely for exercises and walking practice

DISCHARGE - DAY 9 (Feb 12):
- Vision was poor post-surgery, couldn't read menu card
- Experienced aura disturbance - referred to Emergency Eye Doctor (nothing abnormal found)
- Possible lens position change from surgery, aura from exhaustion
- Pulse still raised (>100bpm), still pericarditis evidence
- Driven home (2 hours south) clutching rolled-up towel to chest
- Advised not to lie on side for 6 weeks while sternum heals

DAY 13 (Feb 16) - A&E VISIT:
- Heart pounding and "jumping about" from chest to stomach
- Pulse bouncing 100-160bpm, blood pressure soared
- Called 111, sent to A&E at Worthing Hospital
- ATRIAL FIBRILLATION diagnosed
- Controlled with Bisoprolol 2.5mg, monitored 24 hours
- Kept on low dose Bisoprolol 1.25mg until 6-week review

WEEKS 3-6:
- Calf muscle pains subsided, mobility improved
- Week 5: energy to get dressed, started short walks outside
- Still suffering pericarditis: sharp chest pains when lying down or breathing deeply
- Temperature spikes continuing
- Blood test showed still some inflammation
- Echocardiogram showed no more fluid around heart (good news)
- As mobilised more, pericarditis symptoms subsided

WEEK 8:
- Started Cardiac Rehabilitation (via Heart App due to COVID lockdown)
- Regular walking, becoming stronger
- Week 10: felt more like herself

POST-SURGERY REFLECTIONS:
- Journey was different due to M.E. comorbidities but was a good journey
- Would recommend to others without hesitation

COMPLICATIONS:
- Postoperative hypothermia (stayed on ventilator 11 hours)
- Pericarditis (treated with Ibuprofen, lasted weeks)
- Severe weakness/exhaustion (exacerbated by M.E.)
- Leg/calf pain and severe ankle/feet swelling
- Atrial fibrillation at day 13 (needed A&E, controlled with Bisoprolol)
- Visual disturbances/aura
- Insomnia (3 nights)
- Raised pulse (>100bpm at discharge)

RECOVERY TIMELINE FOR LIVING ALONE PLANNING:
- Could not sit up unaided or walk unassisted until days 5-8
- Needed walking frame assistance initially
- 9 days hospital stay (longer than typical due to M.E.)
- Needed someone to drive home (2 hours)
- Could not lie on side for 6 weeks
- Week 5 before energy to dress and short walks
- Week 8 started cardiac rehab
- Week 10 felt more like herself
