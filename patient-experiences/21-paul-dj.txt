PEARS Patient Story: Paul Dj
Source: https://exstent.com/story/paul-dj/
Condition: Dilated ascending aorta (non-Marfan), hypertension-related, aorta dilated from 47mm to 55mm over 10 years
Location: Melbourne, Australia; surgery at Melbourne Private Hospital
Surgeon: Prof. Alistair Royse
Patient Number: First Australian PEARS recipient
Date of Surgery: September 26, 2018

================================================================================

BACKGROUND:
Engineer with dilated ascending aorta discovered via CT scan after visual disturbances (queried migraines). Not Marfan but similar condition. Over 10 years, aorta dilated from 47mm to 55mm (critical). Consulted Prof Alistair Royse. Traditional options required Warfarin anti-coagulation which he didn't want. Found PEARS through internet research.

SURGERY AND RECOVERY:
- First Australian PEARS recipient, September 26, 2018
- International team: UK (ExoVasc makers), NZ proctor (Adam El Gamel), Australian surgeon (Prof Royse)
- Hospital stay: 6 days
- Walking within hours of returning to ward
- Chest pain well managed with medication
- Coughing was a challenge
- Difficulty sleeping lying down initially - found comfort sitting up
- Had lung infection
- Febrile episode on day 3 (managed with medication)

POST-OP CHANGES:
- Can feel/hear heartbeat at different times, especially lying down at night
- Sternum secured with Sternalock 360 closure system (can be felt when lying down)
- Resumed driving after only 4 weeks (pillow between chest and seat belt)
- Cardiac rehab twice weekly until end of year
- Still taking BP and heart rate medication but NO anti-coagulation drugs

COMPLICATIONS:
- Lung infection
- Febrile episode (day 3)
- Difficulty sleeping lying down
- Coughing challenges

KEY TIPS:
- Exercise muscles after surgery through rehab
- Pillow between chest and seatbelt when driving

KEY MESSAGE: Encourages anyone with dilated ascending aorta to research PEARS as a viable option.
