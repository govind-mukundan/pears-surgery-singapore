PEARS Patient Story: Michael Boecker
Source: https://exstent.com/story/michael-bocker/
Condition: Bicuspid aortic valve with generalized dilation (4.6cm growing to 5.1cm), prior open heart surgery at age 5 (patent ductus arteriosus Botalli)
Location: Berlin, Germany; surgery at AKH Vienna, Austria
Surgeons: Prof. Dr. Kocher, Prof. Dr. Laufer, Dr. Conal Austin (joining from UK), Dr. Osorio
Patient Number: 794
Date of Surgery: April 25, 2023
Age at Surgery: 54 (surgery was 2 days before birthday)
Writing: 6 months post-surgery

================================================================================

BACKGROUND
----------
Bicuspid aortic valve discovered at age 52 during health check-up, along with 4.6cm aortic dilation. Surprising as he had regular heart checks from childhood open heart surgery. Follow-up showed growth to 5.1cm in just one year. Found PEARS through a German forum. Local heart centre dismissed PEARS entirely. Contacted Prof. Dr. Kocher in Vienna who called back within an hour.

SURGERY
-------
Surgery started at 15:09, ended at 18:22. Woke up at 19:00 with respirator tube already removed. Spent night and birthday morning in ICU, released to ward at lunchtime.

HOSPITAL RECOVERY
-----------------
- Felt good overall except "very serious pain in the back" (lasted 2 weeks, only allowed to sleep on back)
- Tubes removed day by day
- Walking with wife around the ward
- Got fever on Saturday (Day 3) which continued for some days - came in afternoons, gone by evening
- Released from hospital on Sunday (Day 8)

POST-DISCHARGE / REHAB
-----------------------
- Stayed 2 days in hotel, then night train back to Germany
- Started post-operative rehab: daily exercises + health education
- Initial check showed pericardial AND pleural effusion
- Got "quite a mix of medicine" for various problems
- Recovery was faster than other patients (attributed to being fit going in)
- 2-week check: pleural effusion gone, pericardial effusion reduced
- CAUGHT COVID-19: Had to leave rehab clinic for home care (symptoms not as bad as first infection in 2022)
- Covid test negative after few days BUT recovery "more or less back to square one"
- Started second rehab locally in Berlin (4 weeks) - "really helped get strength back"

CURRENT STATUS (6 months)
-------------------------
- Back to work full time since mid-July
- 2-week vacation in Mallorca
- Blood pressure optimal, off ALL medicine
- Regular gym, ergometer, restarted running
- Last weekend ran 18km at 6.3kph
- "Back to business as usual"

COMPLICATIONS NOTED
-------------------
- Severe back pain (2 weeks, from sleeping on back only)
- Intermittent fever for several days post-surgery
- Pericardial effusion (fluid around heart)
- Pleural effusion (fluid around lungs)
- Required multiple medications initially
- COVID-19 infection during rehab (set recovery back to "square one")

KEY RECOVERY TIMELINE
---------------------
- Day 0: Surgery (3h 13min)
- Day 1: ICU overnight, ward by lunchtime
- Day 3: Fever started (came and went for days)
- Day 8: Discharged
- Day 10: Train back to Germany
- Weeks 1-2: Post-op rehab, effusions found
- Week 2: Pleural effusion resolved
- ~Week 3-4: COVID infection, rehab interrupted, recovery reset
- Weeks 5-8: Second rehab in Berlin (4 weeks)
- Mid-July (~3 months): Back to full-time work
- 6 months: Running 18km, off all medication

PRACTICAL TIPS FROM THIS PATIENT
---------------------------------
- Get fit before surgery: it will help recovery
- Start rehab promptly if possible
- Keep up with exercises
- Find forums with similar patients for peer support
- "Recovery will include ups and downs but there is a lot of light at the end of the tunnel"

SUPPORT: Wife accompanied to Vienna. Pre-surgery weekend in Vienna together. Hotel stay after discharge. Night train home.
