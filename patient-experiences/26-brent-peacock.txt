PEARS Patient Story: Brent Peacock
Source: https://exstent.com/story/brent-peacock/
Condition: 5.1cm root aneurysm (familial, no known genetic markers), connective tissue disorder history
Location: USA; surgery at London Bridge Hospital, England
Surgeon: Mr. Conal Austin
Date of Surgery: Early May (year not specified, likely 2017)

================================================================================

BACKGROUND:
Active swimmer and surfer. Mother died suddenly from ruptured aortic root aneurysm, aunt died 30 years prior from same, grandfather from ruptured abdominal aneurysm. Discovered asymptomatic 5.1cm root aneurysm via echo and CT. No known genetic markers. In USA, PEARS not yet licensed. Annual rupture chance ~1 in 33. Had to dial down cardiac activity significantly - only swim with heart rate monitor at 125bpm max. Decided to get assessed by Conal Austin in London.

US insurance APPROVED the procedure (after submitting report and pricing from London Bridge Hospital).

SURGERY:
- Arrived a week early to get over jet lag
- Surgery scheduled 8am, under for 2 hours, uncomplicated
- Woken before ICU but no recollection

ICU:
- Waterworks didn't respond well to anaesthetic - up every 30 min trying to pee
- Instead of catheter, waited it out (days before normalizing)
- Used morphine quota in ICU
- Back on ward next day: fine on paracetamol and diclofenac (took for few weeks)

RECOVERY:
- Shocked at weak breathing initially - improved with spirometer
- Quite mobile from day 1
- Day 4: put clothes on, walked outside by hospital
- Left hospital next day (day 5)
- Coughing/sneezing unpleasant - hugging cushion helped
- 3 weeks post-op: cleared to fly back to US (flew back 1 week later)
- 8-hour flight manageable with walking about
- 3 months post-op: check-up with Grayson Wheatley in Nashville, echo and CT - all good
- Root stabilized at ~4.4cm (from 5.1cm)
- Back swimming, chest wall still a bit tender from rib action
- Planning to surf again

COMPLICATIONS:
- Urinary retention post-anaesthetic (resolved without catheter after several days)
- Weak breathing initially

NOTE FOR LIVING ALONE: Traveled from US to London alone for surgery. Managed recovery in London for ~4 weeks before flying home. Shows international travel and solo recovery is feasible.
