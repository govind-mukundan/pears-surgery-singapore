PEARS Patient Story: Jay Benedict
Source: https://exstent.com/story/jay-benedict/
Condition: Enlarged aortic root 5.1cm (non-Marfan), mild aortic valve leakage, sarcoidosis
Location: London, UK; surgery at St Thomas' Hospital
Surgeon: Mr. Conal Austin
Age: 66
First non-Marfan PEARS patient and oldest at time

================================================================================

BACKGROUND:
66-year-old married man, self-employed, fit and active. No Marfan. Discovered enlarged aortic root (5.1cm) incidentally during testing for sarcoidosis. Monitored for 8 years - stable. Then started creeping up 1mm/year. At 5.3cm with mild valve leakage, approaching danger zone of 5.5cm. Traditional valve-sparing repair sounded too invasive (8 hours, clinically dead on bypass). Wife found Tal's TED talk. Contact with Tal and Prof. Treasure. Referred to Conal Austin. Became first non-Marfan and oldest PEARS recipient.

SURGERY TIMELINE:
Night before: Temazepam for sleep. New pyjamas.
Surgery day: Outside theatre 8:20am. Under anaesthesia.
1 hour post-op: In Overnight Intensive Recovery (OIR). Not yet awake. Propofol and Fentanyl.
1:00pm: Starting to wake - they withdraw drugs as "only pain will bring you round - oh boy!"
4:00pm: Fully awake, breathing tube removed. Sore throat "like never before."
7:00pm: Wife visiting. "I was back in the room."

IMPORTANT NOTE ON OIR: Booklet says most remember nothing - Jay says this is "completely untrue" based on talking to other patients. He remembered squeezing wife's hand, saying "I love you" in sign language, and asking surgeon for a pint.

DAY 1: Transferred to HDU. Feeling "run over by a steam roller that turned round and came back."

DAY 2: Made to sit in chair often. Coughing and deep breathing essential. Self-administered morphine. Went briefly into ATRIAL FIBRILLATION - tried beta blockers then stronger drug therapy, kicked in around midnight (no cardioversion needed).

DAY 3: Chest drain removed (pressed morphine clicker first). Neck line removed. Switched to paracetamol only. Bowels working fine - wife insisted on prunes, apricots and figs from moment he woke up.

DAY 4: Catheter out. Real toilet. Back on main ward. Portable ECG monitoring.

DAY 5: Discharged (Boxing Day!).

HOME RECOVERY:
- Good food, tons of rest/sleep, daily walks vital
- Learning to sleep on back was HARDEST adjustment
- Sleeping at 45 degrees with triangular pillow helped
- Needed bench seat for bath and help to bathe/get in-out of bed for ~10 days
- Slow and steady, every day better
- Off meds after 14 days (BP dropping too low causing dizziness)

COMPLICATIONS:
- Atrial fibrillation (day 2, controlled with medication)
- Severe sore throat from breathing tube
- Low blood pressure when on meds (dizziness)

PRACTICAL TIPS FOR LIVING ALONE:
- Need help bathing and getting in/out of bed for ~10 days
- Bench seat for bath essential
- Triangular pillow for 45-degree sleeping
- Prunes, apricots, figs to keep bowels moving (avoid constipation)
