PEARS Patient Story: Richard Morris
Source: https://exstent.com/story/richard-morris/
Condition: Congenital bicuspid aorta (initially thought to be rheumatic heart murmur), aortic aneurysm
Location: Waikato, New Zealand; surgery at Waikato Hospital
Age: Nearly 70 (oldest patient at time, 6th in southern hemisphere)
Date of Surgery: February 23, 2017

================================================================================

BACKGROUND:
Congenital bicuspid aorta found in early thirties (was thought to be rheumatic murmur). Monitored for decades with no changes until late sixties when aortic aneurysm developed. Local hospital recently accredited for PEARS. Aortic valve was NOT replaced.

SURGERY AND RECOVERY:
- Admitted Feb 22, operation Feb 23
- By next day: sitting in chair and walking to bathroom
- By Feb 26: walking down ward corridor
- Discharged Feb 28 (total: 5.5 days - "amazing")
- NO NEED FOR PAIN KILLERS after discharge - not even Paracetamol
- No pain at incision site
- Only discomfort: ribcage and along spine (flexed during procedure)

COMPLICATIONS: Minimal for this patient.

TIPS FOR OTHERS:
1. Talk to medical staff - don't be confused silently, ask questions
2. Strong painkillers post-op may cause VIVID NIGHTMARES - tell staff if this happens, they can help
3. Keep a cushion or rolled up towel close for chest support when coughing/sneezing
4. Try NOT to sleep on your side for quite some time - back reduces ribcage strain
5. Keep busy but don't over-reach or lift things. When discomfort subsides, still be careful

NOTE: One of the oldest patients and one of the smoothest recoveries reported. No pain killers needed post-discharge is unusual.
