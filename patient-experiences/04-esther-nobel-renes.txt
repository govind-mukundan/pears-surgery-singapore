PEARS Patient Story: Esther Nobel-Renes
Source: https://exstent.com/story/esther-nobel-renes/
Condition: FTAAD (Familial Thoracic Aortic Aneurysm and/or Dissection), aorta 4.5cm
Location: Netherlands; surgery at AMC Amsterdam
Patient Number: 977
Date of Surgery: February 1, 2024
Writing: 3 months post-surgery

================================================================================

BACKGROUND
----------
Physical education teacher for 20+ years. Active in watersports. Brother and father diagnosed with dilated aorta, prompting her own check. Dilation at 4.5cm initially. Had to restrict exercise to max heartrate of 125 bpm. Brother had successful PEARS in April 2023 (with "a few complications" but recovered). Brother had prior TIA (carotid artery dissection) which changed intervention threshold to 4.5cm.

PRE-SURGERY PREPARATION
------------------------
Read stories on exstent.com. Yoga, walking, cycling helped physically and mentally. Children also examined (clear - which gave her peace of mind).

HOSPITAL RECOVERY
-----------------
- ICU for 1 day, then transferred to ward
- Pain was under control
- Walking on the ward within 1 day
- No complications
- Discharged after 6 days

HOME RECOVERY
-------------
"Through trial and error my recovery is going well. Sometimes you want to do more than you are able." Working toward full recovery at 3 months post-surgery. Relief that the dilation is solved.

COMPLICATIONS: None reported for her surgery. Brother had "a few complications" with his PEARS (not detailed).

KEY TIMELINE
------------
- Day 1: ICU
- Day 2: Ward, walking
- Day 6: Discharged
- 3 months: Working toward full recovery

SUPPORT: Husband involved, children aware. Hospital stay 6 days.
