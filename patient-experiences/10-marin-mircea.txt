PEARS Patient Story: Marin Mircea
Source: https://exstent.com/story/marin-mircea/
Condition: Ascending aortic diameter 54-56mm, hypertension, no Marfan diagnosis
Location: Romania; surgery at IKEM Institute, Prague, Czech Republic
Surgeon: Professor Jan Pirk
Patient Number: 49th at IKEM
Date of Surgery: July 23, 2021
Age at Surgery: 52
Writing: ~3 months post-surgery

================================================================================

BACKGROUND
----------
Former performance judo athlete. In Dec 2018, sudden hypertension (200/100). April 2019 echocardiography detected 5cm ascending aorta. Last echo in 2006 showed 3cm. By July 2021: aorta 54-56mm. Romanian surgeons only offered Bentall procedure (mechanical valve + tube). Wife found PEARS through article about "British engineer who designed his own prosthesis." No doctor in Romania recommended PEARS; went on own initiative to IKEM Prague.

SURGERY DAY (July 23, 2021)
----------------------------
- Hospitalized July 22, single room, blood samples, shaved at 10pm
- Woken at 5:30, shower, transported to operating room
- In less than 5 minutes OR filled with staff
- Received anaesthesia, woke as if from "normal sleep"
- Had thick hose (breathing tube) in mouth - "didn't bother me at all"
- Connected to many devices, thin hoses in stomach and neck, urine tube
- WONDERED WHY NO PAIN in body
- Constantly monitored by nurse in ICU

DAY 2 (July 24)
----------------
- Breathing tube removed - no unpleasant sensations
- Hoses still in stomach, throat; urine tube present
- Automatic dispensers above head (painkillers/medicines)
- Moved to another room with his luggage
- Got phone - talked to wife and nephew, told them "nothing hurt"
- Ate first meal, drank coffee
- Called friends, had dinner, restful sleep

DAY 3 (July 25)
----------------
- Medical visit, asked about pain - told operation was OK
- Still wondering why nothing was hurting
- Moved to 3-bed room with a roommate
- Got rid of urine tube
- Helped to shower, first walk down hall (supervised)
- "Fantastic - walking, no pain, a little dizziness"
- Spent day on phone and watching TV

DAY 4 (July 26)
----------------
- More medical visits, walking in hallway, shaved himself
- Visited by Professor Pirk who gave him 2 photos (aorta before and after)
- Visited by nephew

DAY 5 (July 27)
----------------
- Morning weighing: 74.5kg (up from 71kg at admission - fluid retention)
- Walks, TV
- Visited by wife - "pleasantly surprised how good he looked and no pain"

DAY 6 (July 28)
----------------
- Normal day with walks, TV
- Notified leaving next day

DAY 7 (July 29)
----------------
- Shower, bandage and corset changed, last catheters removed from neck
- Said goodbye to staff, left to rented apartment in Prague

POST-DISCHARGE IN PRAGUE (to Aug 12)
--------------------------------------
- Stayed in Prague until Aug 12 (20 days total from admission)
- Recovery went well, daily walks with wife
- Minor events: a few episodes of arrhythmia (max pulse 80, stabilized 60-70)
- Max temperature 38°C (mostly 36-37°C range)
- Blood pressure 110/70 to max 120/80 WITHOUT medication
- NO PAIN
- Slept in armchair with legs outstretched and head up to avoid turning
- Sweating a lot, changed every 2 hours at night
- Wore corset (still wearing at 3 months)

FLIGHT HOME (Aug 12)
---------------------
- Pleasant flight without pain, wearing corset
- Continued daily walks at home ("body would go without limit")
- First follow-up checks at IKEM Prague scheduled for November

COMPLICATIONS NOTED
-------------------
- A few episodes of arrhythmia (minor, stabilized)
- Low-grade fever (max 38°C)
- Weight gain from fluid retention (71 to 74.5kg)
- Sweating heavily at night (requiring clothing changes every 2 hours)
- Dizziness on first walk
- Remarkably: REPORTED NO PAIN throughout entire experience

KEY RECOVERY TIMELINE
---------------------
- Day 1: ICU, breathing tube, no pain
- Day 2: Tube removed, moved to room, eating, calling friends
- Day 3: Walking, shower, minimal dizziness
- Day 4-6: Increasing activity
- Day 7: Discharged to apartment
- Days 7-20: Prague recovery with wife, daily walks
- Day 20: Flight home to Romania
- 3 months: Still wearing corset, daily walks, first checks upcoming

SUPPORT: Wife accompanied (found PEARS for him). Nephew visited. Rented apartment in Prague for recovery. Family present.
