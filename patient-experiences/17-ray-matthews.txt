PEARS Patient Story: Ray Matthews
Source: https://exstent.com/story/ray-matthews/
Condition: Marfan Syndrome, aorta 4.5cm
Location: Houston, Texas, USA; surgery at London Bridge Hospital, England
Surgeon: Mr. Conal Austin
Date of Surgery: September 23, 2020
Age: 40

================================================================================

BACKGROUND:
Known Marfan since age 14. Taking 3 blood pressure medications. Aorta 4.5cm by 2019. US insurance initially denied but approved on appeal. Traveled alone to London, quarantined 2 weeks.

SURGERY (Sept 23, 2020):
- Scheduled 7pm, lasted ~2 hours, successful
- Spoke with wife post-op but no recollection

DAY 1: Woke in ICU - "getting hit by a truck." Drugs for pain but still feel it. Tubes limit movement. Got up briefly. Very weak.

DAY 2: Still ICU. Pain in back/shoulders/neck. Zero appetite, nauseous. Walked ~10 min (exhausting). DEPRESSION hit - questioning recovery. Tubes removed. Moved to regular ward.

DAY 3: More appetite, less pain, still down. SHOWER WAS TURNING POINT - "like everything changed." Walked more than prior 2 days combined.

DAY 4: Feeling much better. Walked 30+ min. Sleeping/eating better.

DAY 5 - DISCHARGE: Walked 45-60 min. Up/down stairs. Taxi to rented London flat.

AFTER: Walking daily. Stayed London ~2 more weeks before cleared to fly. 3 weeks post-op recovering well. Planned return to work at 6 weeks.

COMPLICATIONS: Nausea, depression (unexpected), back/shoulder/neck pain.

NOTE: Traveled alone, managed recovery in rented flat - shows solo recovery IS possible with remote medical support.
