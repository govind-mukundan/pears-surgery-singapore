PEARS Patient Story: James Money
Source: https://exstent.com/story/james-money/
Condition: Aortic root 4.9cm, ascending aorta 5.1cm (non-Marfan), ectopics
Location: UK; surgery at London Bridge Hospital
Surgeon: Mr. Conal Austin
Date of Surgery: January 24, 2020

================================================================================

BACKGROUND:
Experienced unusual ectopic/PVC sensations in chest while picking up suitcase in Oct 2018. Very active lifestyle with gym 3x/week. GP found inverted T waves suggesting possible heart attack (wasn't). Referred to Dr. Riyaz Somani for electrophysiology. Stress MRI discovered aortic root 4.9cm and ascending aorta 5.1cm. Was devastated upon learning the extent of dilation.

Extensive research led to PEARS as Choice #1. Contacted Tal Golesworthy via Facebook Messenger, received comprehensive answers. Eventually got private insurance (Healix) to cover PEARS after initially being denied (appealed successfully).

PRE-OP:
- PEARS CT scan before Christmas
- Operation date set for January 24
- Pre-op assessment by Sister Suzanne O'Connor - thorough and reassuring
- Tal in regular contact offering advice and support

SURGERY DAY (January 24, 2020):
- Arrived at London Bridge Hospital 8am
- Visited by ward doctor, anaesthetist, cardiac sister, nurse in charge, Mr. Austin
- Given sedative, taken to theatre
- Surgery successful

POST-OP:
- Woke up with heavy chest and tight lungs, saw wife's face
- First words: "Have I made it?" - repeated this 5 times with fist bumps
- Pain relief effective, self-regulated Fentanyl button was a godsend
- Slight wobble when chest drain removed (sweaty, hot)
- ICU care was excellent

SATURDAY (DAY 1 POST-OP):
- Moved from ICU back to room
- Pain better than imagined but lungs felt like peanuts
- Oxygen provided, every breath felt earned for 24 hours
- Night seemed to last forever
- Showered and sitting in chair by morning visiting hours - amazed
- Managed few stretches and first steps with physio

SUNDAY (DAY 2):
- Broken sleep, some discomfort
- Nurses responded seconds after call button
- ~5 hours sleep managed
- Wife commented looked much better
- Walking, less pain, symptoms managed effectively

MONDAY (DAY 3):
- Showered, dressed, breakfast independently
- Still some weaker breathing, temperatures when pain relief wearing off, minor visual disturbances
- All symptoms explained by Tal and nurses, all gradually passed

TUESDAY (DAY 4):
- Slightly quicker heart rate and ectopics (reduced as lungs improved)
- Echocardiogram showed no regurgitation now visible (previously had some) - PEARS device securing valve
- Discharge mentioned for Thursday

WEDNESDAY (DAY 5):
- All cannulae and wound dressings removed except chest drain stitch
- Temperatures becoming less severe
- Doing steady hourly laps of cardiac ward with wife

THURSDAY (DAY 6) - DISCHARGE:
- Final checks, pharmacist explained medication
- Sister O'Connor went through next week of rehab
- Car journey home had bumps/potholes that were uncomfortable

HOME RECOVERY (2 weeks post-op):
- Following rehab directions closely
- Every day feeling some progress
- Feeling more hopeful about fully fit self returning

COMPLICATIONS:
- Tight lungs/breathing difficulty (first 24-48 hours)
- Visual disturbances (temporary)
- Temperatures when pain relief wearing off
- Ectopics/quicker heart rate (settled as lungs improved)

KEY QUOTE: "They and all the people involved in making this happen for me have given me a future to look forward to with my family and my health."
