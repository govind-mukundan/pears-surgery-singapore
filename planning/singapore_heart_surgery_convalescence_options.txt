1. SUMMARY OF BEST-FIT RECOVERY MODEL
================================================================================

Recommended model for this user:
- Use a private caregiver/home personal care provider as the core support.
- Add overnight caregiver coverage for the first 2-4 weeks if budget allows.
- Add private nurse visits selectively for wound care, vitals checks, medication review, and early complication screening.
- Use separate part-time cleaning/cooking help if caregiver time is too expensive for household tasks.
- Arrange separate cat-care backup, especially for litter, feeding, and any medical routines.
- Use meal delivery/tingkat to reduce dependence on cooking help.
- Avoid hiring an FDW/MDW unless there is a long-term need beyond post-surgery recovery.

Key reason:
- User lives alone and has pets, but the need is expected to be short-term.
- A full FDW/MDW arrangement has high setup overhead and is not ideal for only 1-3 months.
- A mix of caregiver + meal delivery + pet sitter + optional nurse gives flexibility and lower commitment.

2. RECOVERY TIMELINE ASSUMPTIONS FOR OPEN HEART SURGERY / STERNOTOMY
================================================================================

Use this only if the operation involves sternotomy/open chest. Verify with surgeon.

Typical sternum and functional recovery:
- Weeks 0-2:
  - Highest dependency.
  - Fatigue, pain, poor sleep, reduced mobility.
  - Avoid bending, lifting, pushing, pulling, using arms to get out of bed, and lifting pet litter or heavy groceries.
  - Strong case for daily daytime help plus overnight care.
- Weeks 3-4:
  - May be able to do light work from home if mentally clear and surgeon approves.
  - Still avoid commuting and carrying bags.
  - Daily caregiver help remains useful.
  - Overnight care may still be justified if living alone.
- Weeks 5-6:
  - Borderline transition phase.
  - Some light office-type work may be possible from home.
  - Public transport and carrying laptop are still not ideal because of crowds, sudden jerks, standing, and load.
- Weeks 6-8:
  - Key transition milestone for many sternotomy patients.
  - Hybrid/limited office return may be possible if surgeon approves.
  - Use off-peak travel and avoid carrying laptop on shoulder.
- Weeks 8-10:
  - More realistic for normal commuting and office work, with modifications.
  - Fatigue may still occur.
- Weeks 8-12:
  - Sternum/breastbone continues strengthening.
  - Most daily activities resume gradually.
- 3-6 months:
  - More complete recovery/strengthening; heavy lifting and intense activity only if cleared.

Practical work timeline discussed:
- Fully off or remote only: weeks 0-4.
- Remote/hybrid light work: weeks 4-6.
- Hybrid office return: weeks 6-8, if cleared.
- Full commute + normal routine: around weeks 8-10.
- Laptop strategy:
  - Prefer leaving laptop at office/home, using VDI/remote access, or using a wheeled bag.
  - Avoid single-shoulder laptop bags early.
  - Avoid peak-hour public transport until strength and balance improve.

Planning implication:
- Household support should be strongest in first 4 weeks.
- Cat litter, groceries, laundry, changing bedsheets, mopping, and cooking should be delegated for at least 4-6 weeks.
- Keep support available through at least week 8 if open sternotomy.

4. OPTION: OVERNIGHT CARE FOR FIRST 4 WEEKS
================================================================================

What overnight care usually means:
- A caregiver is present overnight, often 8-12 hours depending on provider.
- Helps with bathroom trips, mobility, water, positioning, and general reassurance.
- Can call emergency services if there is chest pain, collapse, severe breathlessness, or other emergency.
- Not equivalent to ICU or continuous clinical monitoring.
- A non-nurse caregiver should not be expected to interpret complex medical symptoms.

Typical overnight shift examples:
- 10pm-6am (8 hours), as discussed in chat.
- Some providers use 8pm-8am or 12-hour night shifts; confirm.

Cost model 1: using Homage night care estimate
- S$100-S$150/night x 28 nights = S$2,800-S$4,200 for 4 weeks.

Cost model 2: hourly night caregiver
- 8 hours/night x S$27-S$37/hour x 28 nights = S$6,048-S$8,288.
- If using Jaga-Me’s listed day 8-12h tier plus S$2.18/hour night surcharge, rough night hourly would be around S$29.34/hour weekday or higher weekends/PH, excluding exact package handling.
- 8 hours x S$29.34 x 28 nights = about S$6,572 before any weekend/PH differences.
- This highlights why exact overnight quote matters: “sleep-in/night care package” may be much cheaper than hourly active-care pricing.

Combined first 4 weeks with daytime caregiver:
- Daytime weeks 1-2: 5-6 h/day x 14 = 70-84 hours.
- Daytime weeks 3-4: 3-4 h/day x 14 = 42-56 hours.
- Total daytime caregiver hours first 4 weeks: 112-140 hours.
- Daytime cost at S$25-S$35/hour: S$2,800-S$4,900.
- Overnight cost using S$100-S$150/night: S$2,800-S$4,200.
- Total first 4 weeks: approx. S$5,600-S$9,100.
- Prior chat midpoint estimate using S$150/night and S$25/hour was about S$7,300.

Assistance-on-hand estimate:
Weeks 1-2:
- Daytime caregiver: 5-6 hours/day.
- Overnight caregiver: 8 hours/night.
- Total covered: 13-14 hours/day.
- Alone: 10-11 hours/day.
Weeks 3-4:
- Daytime caregiver: 3-4 hours/day.
- Overnight caregiver: 8 hours/night.
- Total covered: 11-12 hours/day.
- Alone: 12-13 hours/day.

Best-fit overnight plan:
- Highest safety: overnight every night for first 4 weeks + daytime support.
- Balanced: overnight nightly for first 2 weeks, then alternate nights in weeks 3-4.
- Cost-minimized: overnight first 7-14 nights only + stronger daytime support.

Questions to ask providers:
- Is overnight care sleep-in, awake, or active monitoring?
- Exact shift hours?
- Is there a bed/rest area requirement for caregiver?
- What happens if user wakes frequently?
- What tasks are included/excluded overnight?
- Can the overnight caregiver assist with cats if needed?
- Who is the escalation contact?
- Are night rates flat or hourly?
- Are weekends and public holidays priced differently?
- Is GST included?

5. OPTION: PRIVATE NURSE / REGISTERED NURSE / ENROLLED NURSE
================================================================================

Best for:
- Wound/incision care and dressing if prescribed.
- Monitoring blood pressure, pulse, oxygen saturation, temperature.
- Medication review/reminders, administration support within scope.
- Post-surgical observation and complication screening.
- Liaising/escalating to doctor/hospital.
- Injections or procedures where prescribed and within scope.
- Teaching caregiver/user how to monitor warning signs.

Not ideal for:
- Routine cooking.
- Deep cleaning.
- Pet care.
- Long unskilled standby if no medical need.

Cost guidance:
- User is not Singapore Citizen/PR, so assume private/unsubsidized rates unless provider confirms otherwise.
- NTUC Health article: average private nurse range from S$25/hour with subsidies to S$150/hour without subsidies.
- Homage lists nursing care from S$26.50/hour, but actual cost depends on care plan, subsidies, package, timing, and service type.
- Practical planning range for selective visits:
  - 1-2 hour nurse visit: request provider quote.
  - Budget placeholder: S$60-S$200 per visit depending on provider, duration, night/weekend, procedure complexity.
- Good cost strategy:
  - Do not use nurse for all-day care unless medically necessary.
  - Use nurse for targeted clinical checks + caregiver for non-clinical time.

Suggested nurse schedule:
- Before discharge:
  - Ask hospital what dressing/wound care is needed at home.
  - Ask whether nurse visits are recommended and for how long.
- Week 1:
  - Nurse 2-3 visits/week or more if wound care is needed.
- Weeks 2-4:
  - Nurse weekly or as prescribed.
- Weeks 5-8:
  - Nurse PRN (as needed) unless complications or ongoing wound care.
- Always escalate to hospital/doctor for:
  - chest pain, severe breathlessness, fainting, fever, wound discharge/redness, sternal clicking/shifting, sudden swelling, or fast/irregular heart rhythm symptoms.

Provider candidates:
- Homage private nurse/home nursing.
- Jaga-Me home nursing/home medical care.
- NTUC Health private nurse.

7. OPTION: FDW / MDW / LIVE-IN HELPER
================================================================================

Conclusion from chat:
- Not a good fit for a purely temporary 1-3 month convalescence unless user wants long-term live-in help anyway.
- Practical commitment tends to be around 1-2 years because of work permit, contract, agency fees, onboarding, insurance, levy, and repatriation.
- Legally, MOM allows termination according to contract notice/payment in lieu, but early termination still involves administrative duties and costs.
- Transfer helper may reduce onboarding time, but still not designed for short-term recovery only.

Official/legal points:
- MOM says a Work Permit is required to hire a migrant domestic worker in Singapore.
- MOM says MDW/FDW can terminate employment contract early if required notice is served or payment in lieu is made; employer likewise should give notice as stated in contract.
- MOM says cancel helper’s Work Permit within 1 week of her last day of work / after departure as applicable.
- Standard employment contract references employment period as specified in work permit.

Cost items to consider:
- Salary.
- Monthly levy.
- Food/accommodation.
- Agency fee.
- Insurance/security bond.
- Medical checkups.
- Repatriation/air ticket on termination.
- Time to hire and settle in.
- Employer responsibilities.

8. PET CARE PLAN FOR 2 CATS
================================================================================

Why separate pet planning is needed:
- Caregivers/nurses may not reliably handle cats unless agreed in scope.
- Cat litter can be heavy and bending/lifting is restricted after sternotomy.
- If one cat has a medical condition, arrange trained backup before surgery.
- Sudden pet jumping/pulling or bending to feed/clean can strain sternum.

Minimum pet tasks to delegate for first 4-6 weeks:
- Feeding.
- Water refresh.
- Litter scooping and full litter changes.
- Trash disposal for litter.
- Basic observation of appetite, stool, urination, vomiting, lethargy.
- Medication/insulin only if sitter is experienced and agreed.

Pet sitter plan:
- Trial at least 2-4 weeks before surgery.
- Hire sitter for home visits even if caregiver is present, unless caregiver explicitly accepts cat tasks.
- Prepare written cat instructions.
- Label food/medications.
- Keep extra supplies at waist height, not floor level.
- Avoid lifting large litter bags; pre-portion litter or use smaller bags.
- Consider medical boarding for a diabetic/medically complex cat if home support is unreliable.

User is not Singapore Citizen or PR.

- “I am not Singaporean/PR. Please quote private/unsubsidized rates.”
- “Is GST included?”
- “Are there surcharges for weekends, public holidays, and night hours?”
- “Are there package rates for 4 weeks of nightly care?”
- “Can I book before surgery with a start date dependent on hospital discharge?”
- “What happens if discharge date changes?”

11. SCENARIO COST MODELS
================================================================================

Scenario A: Caregiver-only staged plan, no overnight
Use case:
- Lower cost.
- User is comfortable being alone overnight.
- Emergency contact/call button available.
Schedule:
- Weeks 1-2: 5-6 hours/day.
- Weeks 3-6: 3-4 hours/day.
- Weeks 7-10: 10-12 hours/week.
Estimated cost:
- S$4,850-S$8,540 for 10 weeks using S$25-S$35/hour.
Assistance:
- Weeks 1-2: 5-6 hours/day.
- Weeks 3-6: 3-4 hours/day.
- Weeks 7-10: 10-12 hours/week.
Risks:
- Alone at night.
- Need emergency plan.
- May still need nurse visits if wound care is required.

Scenario B: Overnight care for first 4 weeks + daytime caregiver
Use case:
- Best fit if user lives alone and wants night safety net.
Schedule:
- Weeks 1-2: daytime 5-6 hours/day + overnight 8 hours/night.
- Weeks 3-4: daytime 3-4 hours/day + overnight 8 hours/night.
- Weeks 5-10: transition to daytime-only per need.
Estimated first 4 weeks:
- Daytime: S$2,800-S$4,900.
- Overnight: S$2,800-S$4,200 if using S$100-S$150/night package.
- Total first 4 weeks: S$5,600-S$9,100.
Assistance:
- Weeks 1-2: 13-14 hours/day covered.
- Weeks 3-4: 11-12 hours/day covered.
Risks:
- Exact overnight pricing may be much higher if billed hourly active-care.
- Caregiver is not nurse.

Scenario C: Balanced safety/cost
Use case:
- Good compromise.
Schedule:
- Weeks 1-2: overnight every night + daytime 4-5 hours/day.
- Weeks 3-4: overnight alternate nights + daytime 3 hours/day.
- Weeks 5-8: caregiver 3 hours/day or 3-5 days/week.
- Nurse check: 1-2x/week initially or as prescribed.
Estimated cost:
- Requires provider quotes, but likely lower than full 4-week overnight.
- Prior planning target: around S$5k-S$6.5k for first month if careful with package rates and shorter daytime visits.
Risks:
- Some nights alone in weeks 3-4.
- Need emergency contact and cat backup.

Scenario D: High-clinical-support model
Use case:
- More complex surgery, wound care, complications, high anxiety living alone.
Schedule:
- Overnight caregiver 2-4 weeks.
- Daytime caregiver 4-6 hours/day initially.
- Nurse visits 2-3x/week week 1, then weekly.
Estimated cost:
- Higher than Scenario B; obtain provider package quotes.
Risks:
- More expensive.
- Need clear division between nurse vs caregiver vs cleaner.

12. PRE-SURGERY PREPARATION CHECKLIST
================================================================================

8-12 weeks before surgery:
- Confirm surgery type: sternotomy/open chest vs minimally invasive.
- Ask surgeon for expected restrictions: lifting limit, driving, public transport, return to work, wound care.
- Ask hospital discharge planner for home care recommendations.
- Shortlist 2 caregiver providers and 1 backup.
- Shortlist pet sitter and do meet-and-greet.
- Decide meal delivery/tingkat trial.
- Ask employer for remote work/hybrid plan for at least 6-8 weeks if sternotomy.

4-6 weeks before surgery:
- Trial caregiver or cleaner.
- Trial pet sitter.
- Trial meal delivery.
- Reorganize home:
  - put cat food/litter at waist height;
  - pre-portion litter;
  - remove trip hazards;
  - put chargers/phone/water/meds near bed;
  - use shower chair if needed;
  - stock light groceries.
- Book tentative caregiver schedule with flexible start date.

1-2 weeks before surgery:
- Confirm discharge contact process.
- Confirm who receives you at home.
- Confirm first 72 hours after discharge coverage.
- Prepare emergency list and medication list.
- Arrange cat supplies for 4-6 weeks.
- Clean home before admission.
- Prepare low-effort meals/snacks.

At hospital discharge:
- Get written instructions:
  - wound care;
  - bathing;
  - medication schedule;
  - pain plan;
  - lifting restriction;
  - red flags;
  - follow-up dates;
  - cardiac rehab dates;
  - emergency contact number.
- Confirm whether nurse visits are required.
- Activate caregiver start date.

================================================================================
13. EMERGENCY / ESCALATION PLAN
================================================================================

Minimum setup:
- Phone always within reach.
- Charger at bed.
- Emergency number 995 saved.
- Provider escalation number saved.
- Neighbor/friend/colleague backup contact.
- Copy of hospital discharge summary accessible.
- Medication list printed.
- Cats’ care instructions printed.

Red flags requiring urgent medical attention:
- Chest pain/pressure not clearly explained by wound pain.
- Severe shortness of breath.
- Fainting/collapse.
- Confusion.
- Fever.
- Wound redness, swelling, pus/discharge, opening, or worsening pain.
- Sternal clicking, shifting, or instability.
- Sudden leg swelling or severe calf pain.
- Fast/irregular heartbeat with symptoms.
- Excessive bleeding or black stools if on blood thinners.

14. DATA FIELDS FOR LLM SHORTLISTING
================================================================================

Selection criteria:
- Total cost for first 4 weeks.
- Total cost through 8-10 weeks.
- Ability to provide same caregiver.
- Overnight package availability.
- Experience with post-cardiac-surgery patients.
- Ability to support cats/litter or willingness to work around cats.
- Nurse add-on availability.
- Weekend/PH surcharge.
- Cancellation flexibility.
- Minimum booking duration.
- Lead time.
- GST included.
- Private/unsubsidized pricing.
- App/phone support reliability.
- Emergency escalation protocol.

Recommended initial outreach list:
1. Homage: ask for 4-week overnight + daytime caregiver quote, plus optional nurse visits.
2. Jaga-Me: ask for 4-week day/night caregiver quote using official hourly rates and night surcharge; ask about post-cardiac experience.
3. NTUC Health: ask about private nurse/home care availability and lead time; ask for non-subsidized quote.
4. Active Global: ask about short-term post-op/interim caregiver or live-in caregiver package.
5. Aseana: ask about short-term/ad hoc caregiver minimum 6 days and rates.
6. Pawshake/PetBacker: find a cat sitter comfortable with medical routines; do trial.
7. Meal provider: trial one healthy/low-sodium-ready option.

Suggested message to care providers:
"Hi, I am planning to be discharged after elective heart surgery in a few months and may need support for 4-8 weeks: daytime help with showering/mobility, meal setup, light cleaning, medication reminders, and possibly overnight care for the first 2-4 weeks. I have cats, so please let me know whether caregivers can work in a home with pets and whether light pet tasks such as feeding/litter can be included or excluded. Please provide private quote options, minimum booking duration, weekend/night surcharge, and availability for the same caregiver."

Suggested message to pet sitters:
"Hi, I am planning elective surgery and need a reliable cat sitter for 2 cats during recovery. Tasks include feeding, water, litter cleaning, and observing appetite/stool/urination. One cat may require medical monitoring/medication depending on the final plan. Are you comfortable with cats needing medical routines, and can we do a trial visit before surgery?"
