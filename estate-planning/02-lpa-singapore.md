# Lasting Power of Attorney (LPA) Steps - Singapore

## Goal

Set up an LPA so a trusted person can make decisions for you if you lose mental capacity.

## What LPA Covers in Singapore

The LPA generally covers two decision areas:

- Personal welfare decisions.
- Property and affairs decisions (financial matters).

For your situation (bank and stock assets), the property/affairs section is especially important.

## Step 1: Decide Who to Appoint

Choose:

- Donee (primary decision-maker).
- Replacement donee(s).

Decide whether multiple donees act:

- Jointly (all must agree), or
- Jointly and severally (can act independently), or
- Mixed basis by decision type.

## Step 2: Choose Scope and Safeguards

Define practical limits, for example:

- Access to banking and brokerage operations.
- Ability to pay medical and living expenses.
- Reporting expectations to family/trusted contacts.
- Restrictions for gifts/transfers unless specifically allowed.

## Step 3: Prepare Details Before Filing

Prepare:

- Your ID details and donee details.
- Contact details for all parties.
- Clear written preferences/instructions.
- Medical and care preferences in a separate note (optional but helpful).

## Step 4: Complete LPA Application

Use Singapore's official LPA process (Office of the Public Guardian, OPG), then have the LPA certified by an authorized certificate issuer.

Common certificate issuers include:

- Accredited medical practitioners.
- Practicing lawyers.
- Registered psychiatrists.

Check current OPG guidance and fees at the time you file.

## Step 5: Certification Appointment

At certification, be ready to confirm:

- You understand the LPA powers.
- No coercion is involved.
- You understand consequences of the donee's authority.

## Step 6: Registration and Waiting Period

- Submit the certified LPA for registration.
- Track status until registration is completed.
- Keep confirmation records and reference number.

## Step 7: Notify Key People

After registration:

- Provide donee(s) with a copy and practical instructions.
- Tell executor where LPA records are stored.
- Inform key family contacts that LPA exists.

## Step 8: Create an Operations Pack for Donee

Create a separate document containing:

- Bank/broker relationship manager contacts.
- How to locate account statements.
- Bill payment schedule and recurring commitments.
- Emergency medical contacts.

Do not put passwords directly in the LPA.

## Quick Timeline (1-3 Weeks Typical Prep)

- Days 1-3: Choose donee(s), powers, and limits.
- Days 4-7: Draft and review application details.
- Days 8-12: Certification appointment.
- Days 13+: Submit and wait for registration completion.
