# Will & Final Testament Steps (Singapore + India Financial Assets)

## Goal

Create a legally valid will that covers your Singapore and India bank and brokerage assets, names decision-makers, and avoids confusion during probate.

## Step 1: Choose Your Strategy

Use one of these two approaches after legal advice:

- Single comprehensive will (all jurisdictions), or
- Two coordinated wills (one for Singapore assets, one for India assets) with explicit non-revocation language.

For cross-border estates, two coordinated wills are often cleaner operationally, but only if drafted carefully.

## Step 2: Decide Key Roles

Record the following:

- Executor (primary).
- Backup executor.
- Beneficiaries and share split (percentages).
- Alternate beneficiaries (if any primary beneficiary predeceases you).

Pick executors who can handle paperwork in Singapore and India, or appoint one per jurisdiction.

## Step 3: Build an Asset Schedule

Create a list with:

- Bank name, account type, account identifier (masked format for working copies).
- Fixed deposit details (bank, maturity, nomination if any).
- Brokerage/DP account details (broker name, account identifiers).
- Stock holdings summary.
- Any linked cash management account.

Do not include full credentials in the will itself.

## Step 4: Gather Supporting Documents

- Passport/NRIC or identification documents.
- Current address proof.
- Existing nominations for SG and India accounts.
- Beneficiary legal names and IDs (as required by local law).
- List of recurring liabilities (cards, loans, subscriptions).

## Step 5: Draft the Will

Minimum clauses to include:

- Revocation clause for prior wills (or tailored carve-out if using two wills).
- Appointment of executor(s).
- Clear distribution clauses by percentage or named accounts.
- Residuary clause (covers anything omitted).
- Debts/taxes/expenses clause.
- Digital assets instruction clause.

Optional but useful:

- Funeral/burial wishes (non-binding in many places).
- Letter of wishes (separate from will, easier to update).

## Step 6: Validate Tax and Compliance Impact

Ask counsel to confirm:

- Estate duty/inheritance tax implications (if any) in each jurisdiction.
- Capital gains handling on transfer/sale of securities.
- Whether probate/resealing is required for cross-border execution.

## Step 7: Execute Correctly

- Sign according to local legal formalities (witness count and eligibility matter).
- Witnesses should not be beneficiaries.
- Sign all pages if advised by counsel.
- Date the document clearly.

If using two wills, sign both with legal supervision to avoid accidental revocation conflicts.

## Step 8: Store and Communicate

- Keep original signed will(s) in secure physical storage.
- Provide executor with location details and lawyer contact.
- Keep a sealed or encrypted copy of the asset schedule and account contacts.

## Step 9: Update Trigger Rules

Review immediately after:

- Major account changes or new broker/bank.
- Beneficiary or relationship changes.
- Relocation, citizenship, or residency status change.
- Material portfolio value change.

## 2-Week Execution Plan

- Day 1-2: Complete asset schedule and role decisions.
- Day 3-5: Get SG + India legal review and strategy choice (single vs dual wills).
- Day 6-8: Draft and revise.
- Day 9-10: Execute and witness.
- Day 11-14: Store originals and brief executor.
