# Home Recovery & Support Guide (Living Alone)

Recovering from PEARS surgery while living alone will require careful planning. The patient stories make it abundantly clear that **you will need assistance** for at least the first two weeks at home. Because the surgeon must cut through your sternum (breastbone), your upper body mobility and strength will be severely restricted.

Here is a breakdown of the challenges you will face and the support you must arrange based on the experiences of 54 other patients.

## 1. The Critical First Two Weeks
During this period, your sternum is very unstable, and your energy levels will be low.

*   **Getting In and Out of Bed:** Because you cannot use your arms to push yourself up, sitting up from a lying position is very difficult. Patient Jay noted he needed physical help getting in and out of bed for the first 10 days. 
*   **Bathing/Showering:** Stepping over a bath edge or standing for a long time can be dangerous. A bath bench or shower seat is highly recommended, and you may need someone nearby in case you feel dizzy.
*   **Dressing:** Reaching over your head or bending down to tie shoes will be painful. **Tip:** Buy button-up shirts, loose-fitting trousers, and slip-on shoes before your surgery.

## 2. Long-Term Restrictions (Weeks 2 to 8)
Even after you regain your energy and can walk miles a day, your sternum is still fusing back together.

*   **Strict Lifting Bans:** You are absolutely forbidden from lifting heavy objects for 6 to 8 weeks. Patients note they were told not to lift anything heavier than a half-filled kettle of water.
*   **Household Chores:** Activities that require pushing, pulling, or reaching are off-limits. Patient Kathy noted she could not use the vacuum cleaner. You will need someone to do your laundry, take out the trash, and clean your home.
*   **Reaching:** Do not store essential items in high cupboards. Patient Jeni felt a "huge pain" simply reaching up for a box. Move all daily items (plates, mugs, food) to waist level before you go to the hospital.

## 3. Mandatory Home Setup

Before you leave for the hospital, you must prepare your sleeping arrangements:
*   **The Sleeping Angle:** You will not be able to sleep on your side for 4 to 6 weeks. You must sleep on your back.
*   **V-Shaped Pillow:** Nearly every patient recommends buying a large V-shaped or triangular wedge pillow. You will need to sleep propped up at a 45-degree angle initially.
*   **The "Coughing Towel":** Coughing or sneezing will be very painful on your chest. Keep a tightly rolled-up towel nearby at all times to clutch against your chest when you need to cough; it provides necessary counter-pressure.

## 4. Driving and Transportation
*   You will not be allowed to drive for **6 weeks**. You will need to arrange rides for any follow-up appointments, grocery shopping, or social visits.
*   When riding as a passenger, patients recommend placing a small pillow between your chest and the seatbelt for comfort.

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Action Plan:** Because you live alone, you must arrange for a family member, friend, or professional carer to stay with you—or at least visit for several hours every day—for the first **10 to 14 days** after you are discharged. You cannot safely manage the physical restrictions of sternum recovery entirely on your own during this window.
